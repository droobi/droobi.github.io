About Me

I am currently a Graduate Research Assistant in Prof. Mustafa Mohamad's Research Group. Prior to joining the Schulich School of Engineering at the University of Calgary, I received my BSc. in Computer Engineering under the supervision of Dr. Raed Qadi in January 2023 from An-Najah National University.

With a passion for applied mathematics, optimization, statistics, probability, machine learning, dynamical systems, and programming, I am deeply interested in leveraging these skills to solve real-world problems in engineering and climate science. I focus on decision-making under uncertainty, data-driven modeling in climate science and manufacturing, stochastic methods in environmental engineering, and scientific machine learning.

Driven by a desire to make a positive impact, I am a self-motivated individual constantly seeking to expand my skillset and deepen my understanding in these areas. I believe that success lies in the ability to adapt and learn quickly, and I am always looking for opportunities to challenge myself and push beyond my current limits.


My research interests include uncertainty quantification, extreme event analysis and prediction, machine learning in dynamical systems, and Bayesian regression methods. I aim to develop innovative techniques to better understand and predict extreme events, quantify and propagate uncertainties, and optimize sensor placement for improved environmental monitoring.

My research spans across fields such as climate science, environmental engineering, and applied mathematics. I am committed to working collaboratively with others to achieve shared goals. Whether working on a research project, developing new algorithms, or building data-driven models, I strive to learn more and make meaningful contributions to the field.
